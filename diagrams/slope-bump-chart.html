Slope / bump chart

About

A slope chart connects two points in time with one line per category, so that rises, falls, and crossovers between a before and after state are legible at a glance. A bump chart extends the idea to ranking changes across many time points, with each line tracing how an item's position rises or falls over a sequence. Both privilege the story of change over the precise values, making them excellent for before/after comparisons and for showing how a ranking reshuffles across periods.

When to use

Use a slope chart to compare two states across a set of categories — survey results before and after a redesign, metrics across two quarters — and a bump chart when you want to show how rankings shift over several successive points in time.

When not to use

Avoid these when many categories cross repeatedly and the lines tangle into spaghetti, or when you need a fine-grained continuous series, where a standard line chart serves better.
